About Punjab Weddings

Punjab is famous for its exuberant and grand wedding celebrations, reflecting the state’s vibrant culture and love for music, dance, and food. Punjabi weddings are not just a union of two individuals but a joyous gathering of families and friends, marked by colorful attire, energetic bhangra and gidda dances, and lavish feasts. The ceremonies blend Sikh and Hindu traditions, with rituals that emphasize family bonds, respect for elders, and the blessings of the Guru. Community participation, traditional songs, and decorative arts make every Punjabi wedding a lively and unforgettable event.

Traditional Punjab Sikh Wedding

Roka/Thaka (Pre-Engagement): The families meet and exchange gifts, marking the formal agreement to the marriage.
Kurmai (Engagement): The engagement ceremony involves the exchange of rings and gifts, with prayers and blessings from elders.
Mehendi and Sangeet: The bride’s hands and feet are adorned with henna, and both families celebrate with music, dance, and singing traditional songs.
Chooda and Kalire Ceremony: The bride is given a set of red and white bangles (chooda) and adorned with kalire (ornaments), symbolizing blessings for her new life.
Anand Karaj (Wedding Ceremony): The main wedding takes place in a Gurdwara, where the couple circles the Guru Granth Sahib four times, signifying their union in the presence of the Guru.
Reception: After the wedding, a grand reception is held with a lavish feast, music, and dance, celebrating the new couple.

Traditional Punjab Hindu Wedding

Roka/Thaka (Pre-Engagement): The families meet and exchange gifts to formalize the relationship.
Engagement (Sagai): The couple exchanges rings and receives blessings from elders.
Mehendi and Sangeet: The bride’s hands and feet are decorated with henna, and both families enjoy music and dance.
Haldi Ceremony: Turmeric paste is applied to the bride and groom for blessings and purification.
Wedding Ceremony: The main rituals include Kanyadaan (giving away of the bride), Mangal Pheras (seven circles around the sacred fire), and Sindoor Daan (applying vermillion).
Reception: The celebrations conclude with a grand feast and dancing, welcoming the newlyweds into the family.
