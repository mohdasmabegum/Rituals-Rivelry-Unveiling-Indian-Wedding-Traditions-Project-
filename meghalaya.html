Traditional Meghalaya Khasi Wedding

Matchmaking and Family Consent: The process begins with both families discussing and agreeing on the marriage, often with the involvement of elders.
Engagement: A simple ceremony is held where gifts are exchanged, and the couple’s union is blessed by the elders.
Main Wedding Ceremony: The wedding is usually held at the bride’s home. Traditional rituals are performed, including the exchange of betel nuts and symbolic gifts. The bride and groom wear traditional Khasi attire, and the ceremony is accompanied by folk music and dance.
Community Feast: After the rituals, a grand feast is organized for family, friends, and the community, featuring local cuisine and celebrations.
Post-Wedding Rituals: The groom may move into the bride’s family home, reflecting the matrilineal tradition, and the couple is welcomed with blessings and festivities.

Traditional Meghalaya Garo Wedding

Proposal and Family Agreement: The marriage proposal is initiated by the woman’s family, and both families discuss and agree on the union.
Engagement Ceremony: Gifts and symbolic items are exchanged between the families.
Main Wedding Ceremony: The wedding is held at the bride’s residence, with rituals that include prayers, blessings from elders, and the exchange of traditional ornaments. The bride and groom wear colorful Garo attire.
Community Celebration: The event concludes with a communal feast, music, and dance, celebrating the new union.
Post-Wedding Customs: The groom often joins the bride’s household, in keeping with Garo matrilineal customs.

Traditional Meghalaya Jaintia Wedding

Family Consent and Arrangements: Both families discuss and agree on the marriage, with elders playing a key role.
Engagement: A small ceremony is held where gifts and symbolic items are exchanged.
Main Wedding Ceremony: The wedding takes place at the bride’s home, with rituals that include prayers, blessings, and the exchange of traditional items. The couple wears Jaintia traditional dress.
Feast and Festivities: A grand feast is organized for the community, with music and dance.
Post-Wedding Rituals: The groom becomes part of the bride’s family, and the couple is welcomed with blessings and continued celebrations.
